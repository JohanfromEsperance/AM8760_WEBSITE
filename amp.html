This includes creating a detailed plan, using CMMS to track asset data, and regularly reviewing and updating the plan based on performance data and changing needs.

Asset Management Plan Template (AMP) using the "6W1H" (Who, What, When, Where, Why, Which, How) approach for thoroughness, and incorporating the "7R" principles (Right information, Right time, Right people, Right tools, Right amount, Right place, Right cost) to ensure that asset management decisions are both optimal and effective.

Asset Management Plan (AMP) Template

1. Executive Summary

  • What: An outline of the AMP’s main objectives and its overall purpose.
  • Why: Justification of the plan in the context of supporting organizational strategic goals.
  • How: Summary of the methodologies to be employed to meet the outlined objectives.

2. Organizational Context

  • What: Detailed description of the organization including its strategic intentions and how these align with asset management goals.
  • Who: Key internal and external stakeholders impacted by or influencing the asset management processes.
  • Why: Rationale behind focusing on specific asset management practices based on organizational goals.
  • Which: Overview of legal, regulatory, and contractual commitments relevant to asset management.

3. Asset Inventory

  • What: Comprehensive list and descriptions of assets under management.
  • Where: Physical and operational locations of these assets.
  • How: Methodology used for categorizing and maintaining asset records.

4. Leadership and Governance

  • Who: Definitions of roles, responsibilities, and authorities of individuals involved in asset management.
  • What: Expectations from the organizational leadership to support asset management.
  • How: Mechanisms established by leadership to show commitment and oversee asset management processes.

5. Performance Objectives and Strategies

  • What: Specific performance objectives for asset management.
  • How: Strategic initiatives to achieve these objectives.
  • When: Timeframes set for the accomplishment of each goal.
  • Which: Risks associated with asset management and plans for their mitigation.

6. Resource Management

  • Who: Detailed skill requirements and responsible entities.
  • What: Necessary resources including personnel, technology, and financials.
  • How: Strategy for deploying and managing resources efficiently.
  • Right tools & Right amount: Assurance that resources are appropriate for the tasks and are used efficiently.

7. Operational Practices

  • What: Detailed descriptions of day-to-day asset management activities.
  • How: Procedures for ongoing operations, change management, and third-party engagements.
  • Where: Identification of operations across different locations.

8. Performance Monitoring and Improvement

  • What: Standards and metrics for monitoring asset performance.
  • When: Schedules for regular reviews and audits.
  • How: Methods employed for continuous monitoring, evaluation, and feedback integration.
  • Right time & Right information: Ensuring timely and accurate data for decision-making.

9. Risk Management

  • What: Identification of potential risks and their impacts on asset management.
  • How: Risk assessment methodologies and mitigation strategies.
  • Why: Importance of risk management in sustaining asset performance and value.

10. Stakeholder Communication

  • Who: Key stakeholders involved in or affected by asset management activities.
  • What: Critical information flows necessary for effective management.
  • How: Communication strategies and methods tailored for different stakeholder groups.
  • When: Timing for regular updates and strategic communications.

11. Financial Planning (direct application of 7Rs)

  • Right cost & Right place: Effective financial management strategies to ensure resources are used where they generate the most value.
