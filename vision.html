Asset Management Policy

Port of Exsto

Document Version: 1.0
Approval Date:12 January 2019
Next Review Date:24 October 2024

1. Purpose

This Asset Management Policy establishes the framework for managing the physical assets of the Port of Exsto. It aims to support the organization's strategic goals by ensuring that assets deliver their required function efficiently, sustainably, and cost-effectively.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all physical assets and asset management activities at the Port of Exsto. It encompasses all phases of asset lifecycle from acquisition, use, and maintenance to disposal.

3. Principles

  • Alignment with Organizational Objectives: Asset management activities will support the achievement of strategic goals set by the Port of Exsto, enhancing organizational performance and stakeholder satisfaction.
  • Sustainability and Environmental Responsibility: Asset management will adhere to sustainability principles by minimizing environmental impact and promoting resource conservation.
  • Risk Management: Asset management decisions will be based on risk assessments to ensure reliability and safety while optimizing performance and cost.
  • Continuous Improvement: The asset management system will be subject to continuous review and improvement, leveraging innovations and best practices.
  • Compliance and Standardization: All asset management activities will comply with legal, regulatory, and standard requirements including ISO 55001 and GFMAM guidelines.

4. Objectives

  • To ensure assets are capable of meeting the service delivery requirements at optimal costs.
  • To improve asset investment decisions based on a balance of risk, cost, and performance.
  • To enhance the life cycle management of assets to achieve better sustainability and efficiency.
  • To develop and maintain an asset management culture through training and engagement of all employees.

5. Responsibilities

  • Board of Directors: Approve asset management strategies and policies; ensure alignment with overall organizational objectives.
  • Asset Management Team: Implement the asset management policy; plan and execute maintenance and investment activities.
  • All Employees: Understand and incorporate asset management responsibilities in their roles.

6. Implementation

The implementation of this policy will be guided by the strategic asset management plan (SAMP), which details the tactical actions necessary to achieve the policy objectives.

7. Monitoring and Review

The effectiveness of the asset management policy will be regularly reviewed and audited to ensure it continues to support the Port’s strategic goals. Adjustments will be made based on feedback and evolving best practices.

8. Communication

This policy will be communicated across all levels of the organization. Training and support will be provided to ensure understanding and compliance.

Bulk Material Handling

Bulk Material Handling involves the transport, storage, and control of materials in bulk. This includes equipment like conveyor belts, crushers, transfer stations, and more.

Storage Sheds

Storage Sheds are used for the temporary storage of materials. They protect materials from the elements and provide a controlled environment for storage.

Rail Infrastructure

Rail Infrastructure includes tracks, bridges, and other structures necessary for the operation of rail transport systems. Maintenance and upgrades are crucial for safety and efficiency.

Ship Loaders/Unloaders are crucial for the efficient handling of cargo on and off ships. Regular maintenance ensures safe and timely operations.

Dust Suppression

Dust Suppression systems control dust emissions during material handling processes. They are essential for environmental compliance and worker safety.

Weighbridges

Weighbridges are used to measure the weight of vehicles and their cargo. Accurate measurements are essential for logistics and inventory management.

Wharves, Berths and Jetties

Wharves, Berths, and Jetties are structures on the shore of a harbor where ships may dock to load and unload cargo. They are critical to port operations and require regular maintenance.

×

Breakwaters

Breakwaters are structures designed to protect a shore or harbor from the force of waves. They play a vital role in maintaining calm waters for safe docking and loading operations.

×

Navigational Aids

Navigational Aids include buoys, lights, and other markers that guide ships safely through channels and into port. Regular maintenance ensures their visibility and effectiveness.

×

Channels

Channels are navigable paths through which ships travel. Regular dredging and maintenance ensure that channels remain deep enough for safe passage of vessels.

×

ICT Tangible Assets

ICT Tangible assets include hardware such as servers, computers, and networking equipment. Regular updates and maintenance are required to keep these systems operational.

×

ICT Intangible Assets

ICT Intangible assets include software and data. Ensuring software is up-to-date and data is securely backed up is essential for operational continuity.

Human Resources Management

Human Resources Management (HR) is critical for ensuring the workforce is well-trained, motivated, and operating efficiently. It includes employee training, recruitment, and performance management.

×

Power

The power system includes all elements that provide energy to the port facility, including generators, transformers, and distribution networks. Maintenance ensures a reliable power supply.

Utilities

Utilities include water supply, sewage, and waste management systems. Proper maintenance ensures the availability of essential services and compliance with environmental regulations.
