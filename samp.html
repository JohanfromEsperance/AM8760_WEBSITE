SAMP (Strategic Asset Management Plan) Details

Who: Asset managers, strategic planners

What: Development of a long-term plan for managing port assets

When: During strategic planning and periodic reviews

Where: Asset management and planning departments

With: Strategic planning tools, asset data

How: By developing and implementing a comprehensive asset management plan

Why: To ensure long-term sustainability and performance of port assets

Quality Check:

  • Right person (managers, planners)
  • Right time (planning, reviews)
  • Right place (management, planning departments)
  • Right equipment (tools, data)
  • Right process (developing, implementing)
  • Right work (comprehensive plan)
  • Right reason (sustainability and performance)

Expanded Activities:

  • Develop a long-term strategic plan for managing port assets.
  • Align asset management strategies with organizational goals and objectives.
  • Implement the plan and monitor progress regularly.
  • Review and update the plan based on changing operational needs and performance data.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Follow guidelines from the Institute of Asset Management (IAM) for strategic asset management planning.
  • Case Studies: Review strategic asset management plans from other ports to identify effective strategies.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your strategic plan with industry standards to ensure alignment with best practices.

Strategic Asset Management Plan (SAMP) Template

1. Executive Summary

  • What: A concise summary of the SAMP's objectives and its scope.
  • Why: Explanation of how the SAMP aligns with the broader strategic goals of the organization.
  • How: Brief overview of the methods used to achieve these goals.

2. Organizational Context

  • What: Detailed description of the organization, its strategic objectives, and the assets included under the SAMP.
  • Who: Identification of stakeholders affected by or having an influence on the asset management system.
  • Why: Explanation of the organizational motivations for prioritizing certain asset management practices.
  • Which: Legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations impacting asset management.

3. Scope of Asset Management

  • What: Assets and activities covered by the SAMP.
  • Where: Geographical and operational boundaries related to asset management.
  • How: Methods to manage assets across different locations and departments.

4. Leadership and Commitment

  • Who: Roles and responsibilities of top management and key asset management personnel.
  • What: Expectations and commitments of leadership towards asset management.
  • How: Mechanisms for leadership to demonstrate commitment and ensure resources.

5. Planning

  • What: Specific asset management objectives.
  • How: Strategies and actions planned to meet these objectives.
  • When: Timeframes for achieving specific goals.
  • Which: Risks that could impact the achievement of objectives and the strategies for their management.

6. Support

  • Who: Identification of necessary competencies and responsible personnel.
  • What: Training programs and information systems needed.
  • How: Methods for developing skills and implementing supportive technologies.
  • Right tools: Ensure the appropriate tools and technologies are in place.

7. Operation

  • What: Detailed asset management activities and operational processes.
  • How: Procedures for managing changes and handling outsourcing or contractor management.
  • Where: Operational locations affected by these processes.

8. Performance Evaluation

  • What: Metrics and standards for measuring asset management performance.
  • When: Frequency of evaluations and audits.
  • How: Techniques and tools used for monitoring and analysis.
  • Right time & Right information: Timeliness and accuracy of information for decision-making.

9. Improvement

  • What: Processes for addressing nonconformity and taking corrective actions.
  • How: Strategies for continual improvement and incorporating lessons learned.
  • Why: Importance of improvement for maintaining and enhancing asset management capabilities.

10. Communication and Stakeholder Engagement

  • Who: Stakeholders involved or affected by asset management.
  • What: Information that needs to be communicated.
  • How: Channels and methods for effective communication.
  • When: Timelines for regular updates and feedback mechanisms.

11. Cost and Resource Allocation (applying 7Rs specifically)

  • Right amount & Right cost: Ensuring that resources are used efficiently and costs are controlled.
  • Right place: Allocation of resources where they are most needed to achieve asset management objectives.
