RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) Details

Who: Project managers, team leads

What: Framework for defining roles and responsibilities in asset management

When: During project planning and periodically reviewed

Where: Project management and operations departments

With: RACI matrices, project management software

How: By developing and implementing a RACI framework for asset management activities

Why: To ensure clarity in roles and responsibilities and improve coordination

Quality Check:

  • Right person (managers, leads)
  • Right time (planning, reviews)
  • Right place (management, operations)
  • Right equipment (matrices, software)
  • Right process (defining roles)
  • Right work (clear responsibilities)
  • Right reason (improve coordination)

Expanded Activities:

  • Develop a RACI matrix to define roles and responsibilities for asset management activities.
  • Implement the RACI framework in project management software.
  • Communicate roles and responsibilities to all team members.
  • Review and update the RACI matrix based on project changes and feedback.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Follow guidelines from the Project Management Institute (PMI) for developing RACI frameworks.
  • Case Studies: Review RACI implementations from other ports to identify effective practices.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your RACI framework with industry standards to ensure clarity and coordination.

Narrative: This block involves developing and implementing a RACI framework for asset management activities. This ensures clarity in roles and responsibilities and improves coordination among team members.
