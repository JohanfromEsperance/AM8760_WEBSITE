PM/CM Conversions Details

Who: Maintenance planners, reliability engineers

What: Conversions of Preventive Maintenance (PM) to Corrective Maintenance (CM) and vice versa

When: During maintenance planning and reviews

Where: Maintenance departments

With: CMMS, maintenance records

How: By analyzing maintenance data and determining the need for conversions

Why: To optimize maintenance strategies and improve asset reliability

Quality Check:

  • Right person (planners, engineers)
  • Right time (planning, reviews)
  • Right place (maintenance departments)
  • Right equipment (CMMS, records)
  • Right process (analyzing, determining)
  • Right work (optimizing maintenance)
  • Right reason (improve reliability)

Expanded Activities:

  • Analyze maintenance records to identify trends.
  • Determine the need for PM/CM conversions.
  • Document and implement conversions.
  • Review and adjust maintenance strategies.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Follow guidelines from the Reliability-Centered Maintenance (RCM) methodology for maintenance conversions.
  • Case Studies: Study conversion processes from other ports to identify effective practices.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your PM/CM conversion rates with industry benchmarks to ensure optimization.

Narrative: This block involves converting Preventive Maintenance (PM) tasks to Corrective Maintenance (CM) tasks and vice versa based on maintenance data analysis. This helps in optimizing maintenance strategies to improve the reliability and performance of port assets.
