AM8760 Comprehensive Training Manual

Introduction

Welcome to the AM8760 training manual. This guide will provide comprehensive instructions and insights into the AM8760 board game, focusing on asset management principles aligned with ISO 55000, ISO 55001, and GFMAM Asset Management Landscape. The game simulates the real-world challenges of managing assets in a port environment, specifically the fictitious Port of Exsto. Through this game, players will gain practical knowledge of asset value management, compliance, investment decision-making, human resource management, downtime management, continuous improvement, and general asset management functions across all GFMAM elements.

Asset Management Elements

Asset Value Management

Maintaining the value of assets through regular maintenance and upgrades is crucial for ensuring their long-term performance and reliability. Asset value management involves:

  1. Preventive Maintenance vs. Corrective Maintenance
    • Preventive Maintenance: Scheduled activities to prevent asset failure, extending asset lifespan and reducing unexpected downtime.
    • Corrective Maintenance: Repairing or replacing assets after a failure, which can be more costly and disruptive.
  2. Long-Term Asset Planning and Lifecycle Management
    • Planning for the entire lifecycle of an asset, from acquisition to disposal, ensures optimal use and value retention.
    • Implementing asset management plans that align with organizational goals and budgets.

Downtime Management

Managing asset downtime effectively is essential for minimizing disruptions to operations and maintaining profitability.

  1. Minimizing Downtime
    • Implementing proactive maintenance strategies to identify and address potential issues before they result in downtime.
    • Quick repairs and efficient response to asset failures to restore operations promptly.
  2. Real-Time Monitoring Systems
    • Utilizing condition monitoring and predictive maintenance technologies to track asset performance in real-time.
    • Early detection of issues through sensors and data analytics to prevent prolonged downtime.

Compliance

Ensuring compliance with regulatory and organizational standards is critical for maintaining the safety, reliability, and legality of operations.

  1. Understanding Regulatory Compliance
    • Keeping up-to-date with relevant laws, regulations, and industry standards.
    • Integrating compliance requirements into asset management plans and practices.
  2. Regular Compliance Checks and Audits
    • Conducting routine inspections and audits to verify compliance with safety, environmental, and operational standards.
    • Implementing corrective actions for any identified non-compliance issues.

Investments

Making strategic investments in assets can enhance their performance and efficiency, contributing to overall organizational success.

  1. Evaluating ROI and Investment Decisions
    • Assessing the potential return on investment (ROI) for asset upgrades and new acquisitions.
    • Balancing short-term costs with long-term benefits to make informed investment decisions.
  2. Data-Driven Investment Strategies
    • Utilizing data and analytics to identify investment opportunities that align with organizational goals.
    • Developing investment plans that prioritize high-impact projects with the best ROI potential.

Human Resource Management

Effective HR management is essential for supporting asset management activities and ensuring a skilled, motivated workforce.

  1. Employee Training and Development
    • Providing ongoing training programs to enhance employee skills and knowledge.
    • Encouraging continuous professional development and certification.
  2. Effective Negotiation and Conflict Resolution
    • Managing employee relations and resolving conflicts to maintain a positive work environment.
    • Negotiating fair employment terms and addressing workforce concerns promptly.

Continuous Improvement

Continuous improvement involves regularly reviewing and enhancing asset management practices to achieve better performance and efficiency.

  1. Performance Monitoring and Feedback
    • Tracking and analyzing asset performance data to identify areas for improvement.
    • Gathering feedback from stakeholders to inform improvement initiatives.
  2. Process Optimization and Innovation
    • Implementing changes to optimize asset management processes and workflows.
    • Encouraging innovation and the adoption of new technologies to enhance asset performance.

Risk Management

Risk management involves identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks associated with asset ownership and operation.

  1. Identifying and Assessing Risks
    • Listing potential risks related to asset performance, maintenance, and compliance.
    • Evaluating the impact of each risk on operations and asset value.
  2. Developing and Implementing Mitigation Plans
    • Creating strategies to reduce the likelihood and impact of high-priority risks.
    • Putting in place controls to monitor and manage risks effectively.

Financial Management and Reliability

Ensuring that adequate resources are allocated for asset management activities and maintaining the consistent performance of assets.

  1. Budgeting for Maintenance
    • Developing and managing budgets for preventive and corrective maintenance activities.
    • Ensuring sufficient funds are allocated for high-priority maintenance tasks.
  2. Cost Control and Financial Reporting
    • Monitoring and controlling costs to ensure financial efficiency.
    • Preparing regular financial reports to track performance and identify areas for improvement.
  3. Reliability Activities
    • Scheduling regular preventive maintenance activities to prevent asset failures.
    • Implementing condition monitoring systems to predict and address potential issues.

Whole of Life Cycle Asset Planning

Whole of life cycle asset planning involves managing an asset from acquisition to disposal, considering all costs and benefits associated with the asset over its lifespan. This approach ensures that asset management decisions are made with a long-term perspective, balancing initial costs with ongoing maintenance and operational costs to maximize asset value.

Key Components of Whole of Life Cycle Asset Planning:

  1. Acquisition Costs
    • Initial purchase price
    • Installation and commissioning costs
  2. Operating Costs
    • Energy and utility costs
    • Routine maintenance and inspections
  3. Maintenance Costs
    • Preventive maintenance to extend asset lifespan
    • Corrective maintenance to address failures
  4. Upgrade and Renewal Costs
    • Costs associated with upgrading or renewing parts of the asset to maintain performance and compliance
  5. Disposal Costs
    • Costs related to decommissioning and disposing of the asset at the end of its useful life

Decision-Making in Whole of Life Cycle Asset Planning:

  1. Life Cycle Cost Analysis (LCCA)
    • Evaluating the total cost of ownership over the asset's life
    • Comparing different asset options to identify the most cost-effective choice
  2. Risk Assessment
    • Identifying potential risks associated with the asset
    • Developing mitigation strategies to manage risks effectively
  3. Performance Monitoring
    • Continuously monitoring asset performance to identify issues early
    • Using data analytics to inform maintenance and investment decisions
  4. Strategic Investment Planning
    • Prioritizing investments based on their impact on asset performance and organizational goals
    • Ensuring investments align with long-term asset management strategies

Benchmark Models from IPWEA (Institute of Public Works Engineering Australasia)

IPWEA provides several models and tools for asset management and whole of life cycle planning, including:

  1. NAMS.PLUS
    • A suite of tools and templates for developing asset management plans
    • Provides guidance on life cycle costing, risk management, and performance monitoring
  2. International Infrastructure Management Manual (IIMM)
    • A comprehensive guide to best practices in infrastructure asset management
    • Covers whole of life cycle asset planning, including acquisition, operation, maintenance, and disposal
  3. Condition Assessment and Asset Performance
    • Tools and methodologies for assessing asset condition and performance
    • Helps prioritize maintenance and renewal activities based on asset criticality and condition
