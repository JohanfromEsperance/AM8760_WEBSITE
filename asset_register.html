Asset Register Details and Design guidelines

Who: Data analysts, asset managers

What: Comprehensive list of all assets, including details on ownership, location, condition, and remaining life

When: During asset acquisition, regularly updated

Where: Centralized asset management system

With: Asset management software, databases

How: By creating and maintaining a comprehensive asset register

Why: To ensure all assets are accounted for and organized systematically

Guidleine for developing an ISO 14224-compliant asset hierarchy and asset register:

ISO 14224-Based Asset Hierarchy and Asset Register

Step 1: Define Scope and Objectives

  • Purpose: Clarify why the asset register is essential for reliability and maintenance data management.
  • Scope: Determine the coverage of the asset register as per ISO 14224’s focus areas.

Step 2: Gather and Review Existing Data

  • Existing Data Sources: Compile existing asset information from various sources.
  • Review Standards and Compliance:
    • Clause 4.2 (General Data Requirements): Ensures all asset data meets general standards for data entry and management.

Step 3: Develop Asset Classification System

Clause Reference:

  • Clause 5.1 (Classification System): Describes the requirements for developing a classification system for asset categorization.

Hierarchy Levels: Define the Structure

Level 1: Facility

  • Definition: A major physical entity such as a plant, refinery, or factory.
  • Sub-steps:
    1. Identify Facilities: List all facilities within the organization.
    2. Assign Unique Identifiers: Use a systematic approach to assign unique IDs to each facility.
    3. Document Facility Attributes: Record attributes such as location, capacity, operational status, and environmental conditions.

Level 2: System

  • Definition: A collection of related equipment within a facility that performs a specific function.
  • Sub-steps:
    1. Identify Systems: Determine the main systems within each facility (e.g., power generation, water treatment).
    2. Assign System Codes: Use consistent coding conventions for systems.
    3. Detail System Specifications: Document specifications such as system type, function, and performance parameters.

Level 3: Subsystem

  • Definition: A subset of a system, composed of components that perform specific tasks within the system.
  • Sub-steps:
    1. Break Down Systems into Subsystems: Identify the subsystems within each system.
    2. Assign Subsystem Codes: Create a unique identifier for each subsystem.
    3. Record Subsystem Data: Document subsystem attributes such as configuration, dependencies, and operational conditions.

Level 4: Equipment

  • Definition: Individual units of machinery or apparatus within a subsystem.
  • Sub-steps:
    1. Catalog Equipment: List all equipment within each subsystem.
    2. Unique Equipment IDs: Assign unique identifiers to each piece of equipment.
    3. Capture Equipment Details: Document details including manufacturer, model, serial number, installation date, and maintenance history.

Level 5: Component

  • Definition: Individual parts or elements of equipment that can be individually maintained or replaced.
  • Sub-steps:
    1. Identify Components: Break down equipment into its individual components.
    2. Component Codes: Assign unique codes to each component.
    3. Document Component Specifications: Record specifications such as part number, material, and expected lifespan.

Detailed Sub-Steps for Creating and Defining Levels

Step 3.1: Facility Level

  1. Survey and List Facilities:

    • Conduct a survey to identify all operational and non-operational facilities.
    • Create a comprehensive list of all facilities.

  2. Assign Unique Identifiers:

    • Develop a naming convention that includes facility type and location (e.g., PLT-001 for Plant 1).
    • Use a database or CMMS to maintain and track these identifiers.

  3. Document Facility Attributes:

    • Record essential attributes: name, location, size, operational status, production capacity, and environmental considerations.
    • Ensure data accuracy and completeness through regular audits.

Step 3.2: System Level

  1. Identify Key Systems:

    • Map out major systems within each facility, such as HVAC, electrical, and process systems.
    • Ensure that all systems contributing to facility operations are considered.

  2. Assign System Codes:

    • Develop a systematic coding convention (e.g., SYS-001 for HVAC System).
    • Ensure consistency across all facilities to facilitate data management.

  3. Detail System Specifications:

    • Document system attributes: system type, function, capacity, and performance metrics.
    • Use technical manuals and manufacturer data to enrich system information.

Step 3.3: Subsystem Level

  1. Break Down Systems:

    • Identify subsystems within each main system, such as compressors within an HVAC system.
    • Ensure all critical and non-critical subsystems are documented.

  2. Assign Subsystem Codes:

    • Create a hierarchical coding structure (e.g., SYS-001-SS1 for Subsystem 1 of HVAC System).
    • Maintain a central registry of subsystem codes for reference.

  3. Record Subsystem Data:

    • Capture configuration details, dependencies, and specific operational conditions.
    • Ensure that data is accessible and up-to-date in the asset management system.

Step 3.4: Equipment Level

  1. Catalog Equipment:

    • Develop a comprehensive inventory of all equipment within each subsystem.
    • Regularly update the inventory to reflect changes such as new installations or decommissioned equipment.

  2. Unique Equipment IDs:

    • Use a structured approach to assign unique IDs (e.g., SYS-001-SS1-EQ1 for Equipment 1 in Subsystem 1 of HVAC System).
    • Integrate these IDs into the CMMS for tracking and management.

  3. Capture Equipment Details:

    • Document detailed specifications: manufacturer, model, serial number, installation date, maintenance history, and performance metrics.
    • Use manufacturer documentation and maintenance records to ensure completeness.

Step 3.5: Component Level

  1. Identify Components:

    • Break down each piece of equipment into its maintainable components.
    • Ensure that all critical components are identified, including spare parts and consumables.

  2. Component Codes:

    • Develop unique codes for each component (e.g., SYS-001-SS1-EQ1-CMP1 for Component 1 of Equipment 1 in Subsystem 1 of HVAC System).
    • Maintain a centralized component registry to avoid duplication.

  3. Document Component Specifications:

    • Capture detailed specifications: part number, material, manufacturer, expected lifespan, and maintenance requirements.
    • Ensure that component data is linked to equipment data for seamless integration

Step 4: Define Asset Attributes

Clause Reference:

  • Clause 5.2 (Data Categories): Specifies the mandatory data categories for asset attributes, emphasizing the need for consistency, accuracy, and comprehensiveness in asset information.

Asset Attributes Definition

Asset attributes are specific pieces of information that define and describe the characteristics of an asset. These attributes must be accurately captured and maintained within a central repository (CMMS, EAM system) to ensure effective management and compliance with ISO 14224.

Alignment with Master Data Management

Master data management (MDM) involves creating a single, authoritative view of all critical data in an organization. Asset attributes in an ISO 14224-compliant asset hierarchy must align with MDM practices to ensure data integrity, consistency, and availability across all systems. This alignment involves:

  • Standardization: Establishing standard definitions and formats for asset data.
  • Integration: Ensuring asset data is integrated across various systems with a central governance mechanism.
  • Quality Control: Implementing procedures to continuously clean, validate, and update data.
  • Accessibility: Ensuring data is accessible to all relevant stakeholders while maintaining security and compliance standards.

Key Asset Attributes

  1. Identification Data:
    • Asset ID
    • Serial Number
    • Manufacturer
    • Model
  2. Operational Data:
    • Installation Date
    • Warranty Expiry Date
    • Service Life
    • Criticality
  3. Performance Data:
    • Capacity
    • Efficiency
    • Output Quality
  4. Maintenance Data:
    • Last Maintenance Date
    • Scheduled Maintenance Date
    • Maintenance History
    • Failure Modes
