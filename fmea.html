FMEA (Failure Modes and Effects Analysis) Details

Who: Reliability engineers, maintenance planners

What: Development of strategies and tactics using FMEA

When: During the planning phase and periodically reviewed

Where: Reliability and maintenance departments

With: FMEA templates, risk assessment tools

How: By conducting FMEA to identify potential failure modes and develop mitigation strategies

Why: To proactively manage risks and improve asset reliability

Quality Check:

  • Right person (engineers, planners)
  • Right time (planning, reviews)
  • Right place (reliability, maintenance departments)
  • Right equipment (templates, tools)
  • Right process (conducting FMEA)
  • Right work (identifying failures)
  • Right reason (manage risks, improve reliability)

Expanded Activities:

  • Identify potential failure modes.
  • Assess the impact and likelihood of failures.
  • Develop mitigation strategies.
  • Document and review FMEA results.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Utilize the guidelines from SAE J1739 for performing FMEA.
  • Case Studies: Examine FMEA reports from other ports to identify common failure modes and effective mitigation strategies.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your FMEA results with industry benchmarks to ensure comprehensive risk management.

Narrative: This block involves using FMEA to identify potential failure modes of port assets and develop strategies and tactics to mitigate these risks. By proactively managing risks, ports can improve the reliability and performance of their assets.
