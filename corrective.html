Corrective Maintenance Details

Who: Maintenance teams, reliability engineers

What: Corrective maintenance activities to address asset failures

When: As needed, based on asset condition and performance

Where: Operational sites, maintenance facilities

With: Maintenance tools, CMMS

How: By performing repairs and corrective actions to restore asset functionality

Why: To minimize downtime and ensure asset reliability

Quality Check:

  • Right person (teams, engineers)
  • Right time (as needed)
  • Right place (sites, facilities)
  • Right equipment (tools, CMMS)
  • Right process (repairing, correcting)
  • Right work (restoring functionality)
  • Right reason (minimize downtime)

Expanded Activities:

  • Identify and diagnose asset failures.
  • Perform necessary repairs and corrective actions.
  • Document corrective maintenance activities in CMMS.
  • Review and analyze failure data to prevent recurrence.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Follow guidelines from the Reliability-Centered Maintenance (RCM) methodology for corrective maintenance.
  • Case Studies: Review corrective maintenance practices from other ports to identify effective strategies.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your corrective maintenance practices with industry standards to ensure reliability.

Narrative: This block involves performing corrective maintenance activities to address asset failures and restore functionality. This helps to minimize downtime and ensure the reliability of port assets.
